What Is a Public IP Address?

Your IP address is a unique identifier assigned to your device by your ISP whenever you connect to the internet. Every website you visit, email you send, and video you stream is tied to it. It reveals your approximate location, your ISP, and creates a persistent trail of your online activity.

Two versions exist: IPv4 (e.g. 192.168.1.1) — the classic 32-bit format — and IPv6 (e.g. 2001:db8::1) — the modern 128-bit standard supporting vastly more devices.

Why Should You Hide Your IP?

Your IP reveals your city, your ISP, and links you to everything you do online. Advertisers build behavioral profiles. Your ISP can sell your browsing history. Hackers on public Wi-Fi can intercept unencrypted data. A VPN solves all three by routing your traffic through an encrypted server in a location you choose.

What Is a VPN?

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and the internet. Everything you do online passes through the VPN server — your real IP is replaced with the server's IP. Websites see the VPN's location, not yours.

VPNs add security (encrypt your data on public Wi-Fi), privacy (hide your IP from trackers and your ISP) and access (unlock geo-restricted content). A quality VPN costs $2–5/month. Free VPNs typically sell your data.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is my IP address and why does it matter?
Your IP is a unique number assigned by your ISP when you connect to the internet. It identifies your device to websites and services, revealing your approximate location, ISP name, and browsing patterns.
Can someone find my exact address from my IP?
No. Your IP reveals your city and ISP, not your home address. Only your ISP can link your IP to your account identity, and doing so requires a valid legal request.
What's the fastest way to hide my IP?
Install a VPN (NordVPN or Surfshark cost under $3/month), click connect, and your IP is hidden in under 60 seconds.
Is it legal to use a VPN?
Yes, in most countries. VPNs are legal in the US, EU, UK, Canada, Australia, and most of the world. A small number of countries restrict use. Using a VPN to commit crimes is still illegal.
