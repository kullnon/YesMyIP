Best Free VPNs 2026

James Fournier
Consumer Advocate
April 2, 2026 · 7 min read

Let's be honest: most free VPNs are worse than no VPN at all. They sell your data, inject ads, or are so slow they're unusable. But a few legitimate VPN companies offer free tiers that are actually safe — just limited. Here's what we found after testing 40+ free VPNs.

The Truth About Free VPNs

Running a VPN costs money — servers, bandwidth, engineers, legal compliance. If a VPN is free, the company needs another way to make money. For most free VPNs, that means:

Selling your browsing data to advertisers and data brokers — the exact thing you're trying to avoid.

Injecting ads into your browsing sessions.

Bundling malware with the VPN app.

Using your bandwidth as exit nodes for other users (Hola VPN does this).

Safe Free VPNs (With Limitations)

1. ProtonVPN Free

The only free VPN with no data limit. Servers in 3 countries (US, Netherlands, Japan). Speeds are throttled compared to paid plans, but it's genuinely secure. ProtonVPN is based in Switzerland and has a proven no-logs policy.

Best for: Daily browsing when you can't afford a paid VPN.

2. Windscribe Free

10GB/month free data. Servers in 10+ countries. Good speeds. Windscribe has a solid reputation and doesn't sell data. 10GB is enough for basic browsing but not for streaming.

Best for: Occasional VPN use on public Wi-Fi.

3. Atlas VPN Free

5GB/month. Servers in 3 locations. Now owned by Nord Security (NordVPN's parent company), so it has credible backing. Limited but safe.

Best for: Testing what a VPN does before committing to a paid plan.

Why Paid VPNs Are Worth It

For $2-3/month, you get unlimited data, 100+ country servers, full speed, streaming support, and zero compromises on privacy. The best budget options:

🦈 Surfshark — $2.29/mo, unlimited devices. Best bang for your buck.

👻 CyberGhost — $2.19/mo, 11,500+ servers. Cheapest premium VPN.

🦁 NordVPN — $3.39/mo, best overall protection and speed.

Free VPNs to Avoid

These free VPNs have been caught selling data, containing malware, or deceiving users:

❌ Hola VPN — Uses your device as an exit node for other users. Caught selling bandwidth to botnets.

❌ SuperVPN — Multiple security researchers found critical vulnerabilities and data leaks.

❌ TouchVPN — Logs and shares browsing data with third parties.

❌ Betternet — Contains tracking libraries and serves ads.
