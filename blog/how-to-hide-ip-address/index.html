How to Hide Your IP Address

Sarah Okonkwo
Privacy Researcher
March 20, 2026 · 7 min read

Your IP address is visible to every website you visit, every email you send, and every file you download. It reveals your location, your ISP, and creates a trail that advertisers and your ISP use to track everything you do online. Here are 5 ways to hide it.

Method 1: Use a VPN (Best Option)

A VPN replaces your real IP with the VPN server's IP and encrypts all your traffic. It's the fastest, most reliable, and most complete solution.

Pros: Encrypts everything. Works on all devices. Fast enough for streaming and gaming. Hides your IP from everyone — websites, ISP, hackers.

Cons: Costs $2-5/month. Adds minimal latency (usually unnoticeable).

How to set up: Sign up for NordVPN or Surfshark, download the app, and tap connect. Done in 60 seconds.

Method 2: Use Tor Browser

Tor routes your traffic through 3 random volunteer nodes worldwide, making it nearly impossible to trace back to you.

Pros: Free. Maximum anonymity. Great for sensitive browsing.

Cons: Extremely slow. Unusable for streaming, downloads, or gaming. Many websites block Tor exit nodes. Only protects browser traffic.

Best for: Journalists, whistleblowers, or anyone who needs extreme anonymity and doesn't mind slow speeds.

Method 3: Use a Proxy Server

A proxy routes your browser traffic through an intermediary server, changing your visible IP.

Pros: Free options available. Quick to set up. Can be faster than VPNs for basic browsing.

Cons: No encryption. Only works in the browser you configure it in. Free proxies are unreliable and may log your data.

Best for: Quick, temporary IP changes when security doesn't matter.

Method 4: Use Mobile Data

Switching from Wi-Fi to mobile data gives you a different IP address assigned by your cellular provider.

Pros: Free. Instant. No software needed.

Cons: Uses mobile data. Your carrier still knows your real identity. Not anonymous.

Best for: Quickly getting a different IP to bypass a temporary block.

Method 5: Use Public Wi-Fi

Connecting to a coffee shop or library Wi-Fi gives you that network's IP address instead of your home IP.

Pros: Free. Changes your visible IP.

Cons: Public Wi-Fi is a security nightmare. Hackers can intercept your unencrypted traffic. You need a VPN even on public Wi-Fi.
