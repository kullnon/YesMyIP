What Is a VPN? The Complete Guide

Priya Sharma
Tech Analyst
March 7, 2026 · 8 min read

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and the internet. Instead of connecting directly to websites, your traffic routes through a VPN server first — hiding your real IP address and encrypting everything you send and receive.

How Does a VPN Work?

When you connect to a VPN, three things happen:

1. Your connection is encrypted. All data between your device and the VPN server is scrambled using military-grade encryption (AES-256). Even if someone intercepts your traffic — on public Wi-Fi, for example — they see gibberish.

2. Your IP address changes. Websites see the VPN server's IP address instead of yours. If you connect to a server in London, every website thinks you're in London.

3. Your ISP can't see what you do. Without a VPN, your Internet Service Provider can see every website you visit. With a VPN, they only see encrypted traffic going to the VPN server.

Why Do You Need a VPN?

Privacy: Your ISP tracks every website you visit. In many countries, they can legally sell this data to advertisers. A VPN stops this completely.

Security: Public Wi-Fi networks at coffee shops, airports, and hotels are hunting grounds for hackers. A VPN encrypts your connection so nobody can steal your passwords, emails, or banking details.

Access: Many streaming services, websites, and apps are restricted by location. A VPN lets you connect through servers in other countries to access content that's blocked in your region.

Anonymity: Advertisers use your IP address to track you across websites and build a profile of your interests. A VPN breaks this tracking chain.

What Makes a Good VPN?

Not all VPNs are equal. Here's what matters:

Speed: A good VPN should barely slow your connection. Top providers like NordVPN and ExpressVPN lose less than 10% of your speed.

No-logs policy: The VPN provider shouldn't keep records of what you do. Look for independently audited no-logs policies.

Server network: More servers in more countries means better speeds and more options for unblocking content.

Kill switch: If your VPN connection drops, a kill switch cuts your internet to prevent data leaks.

Price: Quality VPNs cost $2–5/month on long-term plans. Anything free is likely selling your data.

VPN vs Proxy vs Tor

A proxy changes your IP but doesn't encrypt your traffic. It's faster but far less secure.

Tor routes your traffic through multiple volunteer nodes for maximum anonymity, but it's extremely slow — unusable for streaming or large downloads.

A VPN is the sweet spot: fast enough for everyday use, encrypted, and easy to set up.
